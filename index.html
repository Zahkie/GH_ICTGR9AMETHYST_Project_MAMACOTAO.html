Fires are unpredictable and can happen anywhere, making it essential to always be prepared for the worst. Knowing how to prevent fires from starting in your home is just as crucial as having an escape plan and fire protection tools like smoke alarms and fire extinguishers. By taking simple steps to prevent fires, you can protect your loved ones and safeguard your property. Here are some tips that will help you prevent most fire emergencies:


How to Prevent Fire Emergencies:

Avoid overloading electrical outlets		 Overloading electrical outlets with too many appliances or devices can cause electrical fires. Make sure that you do not exceed the maximum wattage of the outlet and use power strips with surge protectors as needed.

Practice safe cooking		 Cooking is a leading cause of home fires. Never leave cooking food unattended, keep flammable materials like towels and paper away from the stove, and be mindful of loose clothing that could catch fire.

Check and maintain electrical appliances		 Faulty wiring and malfunctioning electrical appliances can cause fires. Regularly check electrical cords and outlets for signs of wear or damage and promptly replace any damaged appliances.

Be cautious with smoking materials		 Smoking materials like cigarettes and matches are a common cause of fires. Never smoke in bed, use ashtrays with deep sides, and make sure smoking materials are fully extinguished before disposing of them.

Have working smoke detectors		 Smoke detectors can alert you to a fire in its early stages, giving you time to escape. Make sure to test smoke detectors monthly, change the batteries at least once a year, and replace smoke detectors that are over 10 years old.
